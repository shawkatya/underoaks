<p>
	  While it took some time to get used to the surnames in Piddubtsi, it's become quite simple to glance at records in Latin and transcribe the names into my database. However, beginning in 1905 the records are written in Ukrainian. There is some mental mapping as I read the handwritten Cyrillic, match it to an existing name in Latin, or transliterate a new one, and enter in the database. Discerning "m" from "sh" from "u" from "t" is a fun challenge. I'm glad the records I have began in Latin, otherwise I might not be able to parse handwritten names like "Tymczyszyn". In 1910 they return to Latin.
	  </p>
